## NAIT (National Animal Identification & Tracing)

- **Legislation**: NAIT Act 2012, NAIT Amendment Act 2019
- **Applies to**: Cattle and deer (mandatory). Pigs proposed.
- **NAIT Number**: Unique location identifier (e.g., `NAIT-12345678`)
- **Requirements**:
  - Register all NAIT locations (farms, saleyards, meat processors)
  - Tag animals with approved RFID within 180 days of birth (cattle) or before first movement (deer)
  - Record all animal movements within 48 hours via NAIT system
  - Annual web declaration confirming animal numbers
- **Penalties**: Infringement fees up to $5,000 per offence; court fines up to $100,000
- **Compliance triggers**: Before any animal movement, sale, or slaughter

## Farm Environment Plans (FEP)

- **Source**: National Policy Statement for Freshwater Management 2020, regional council rules
- **Regional variation**: Rules differ significantly by regional council:
  - **Waikato**: Healthy Rivers / Wai Ora — farm-specific nitrogen reference points
  - **Canterbury**: Plan Change 7 — nutrient allocation, land use consent
  - **Bay of Plenty**: Plan Change 10 (Lake Rotorua) — nitrogen discharge allowances
  - **Southland**: Water and Land Plan — winter grazing rules, stock exclusion
  - **Horizons (Manawatū-Whanganui)**: One Plan — nutrient management
  - **Hawke's Bay**: TANK Plan Change — water allocation
  - **Otago**: Plan Change 7 — freshwater quality
  - **Northland**: Regional Plan — stock exclusion, sediment
- **FEP components**:
  - Property map with waterways, critical source areas
  - Nutrient budget (OverseerFM or equivalent)
  - Risk assessment for contaminant pathways (N, P, sediment, E. coli)
  - Good management practices (GMPs)
  - Riparian management plan
  - Stock exclusion plan
- **Audit cycle**: Typically every 1–3 years depending on risk grading (A/B/C/D)

## NES-Freshwater 2020

- **National Environmental Standards for Freshwater**
- **Key rules**:
  - Intensive winter grazing: Max 50 ha or 10% of farm (whichever is greater); slope ≤ 10°; setbacks from waterways
  - Feedlots: Resource consent required for ≥ 18 stock units per hectare
  - Stock exclusion: Cattle, deer, pigs excluded from natural wetlands, lakes, rivers > 1m wide
  - Stockholding areas: Max 90 consecutive days, 180 days/year
- **Critical dates**:
  - Stock exclusion from waterways: July 2023 (existing farms), July 2025 (low slope)
  - Intensive winter grazing limits: Since 2020

## Emissions Trading Scheme (ETS) & He Waka Eke Noa

- **ETS**: NZ Emissions Trading Scheme — forestry blocks can earn NZUs
- **He Waka Eke Noa**: Primary sector climate action partnership
  - Farm-level greenhouse gas reporting (from 2025)
  - On-farm emissions: Methane (CH₄) from enteric fermentation, Nitrous oxide (N₂O) from fertiliser/urine
  - Sequestration: Existing/new forestry, riparian plantings
- **Calculation methodology**:
  - Methane: Stock units × species factor × days on farm
  - N₂O: Nitrogen applied (fertiliser + effluent) × emission factor
  - Sequestration: Forest area × species × age → carbon lookup tables
- **Mitigation options**: Low-emission feeds, nitrification inhibitors, selective breeding, methane vaccines (development), forestry offsets

## Dairy — Fonterra & Processor Relationships

- **Supplier number**: Unique identifier for each supplying farm
- **Milk price components**:
  - Farmgate milk price (per kgMS — kilogram of milk solids)
  - Dividend (for shareholder suppliers)
  - Season: 1 June – 31 May
- **Payment schedule**: Monthly advance payments based on forecast farmgate price; final price confirmed post-season
- **Composition testing**: Milkfat, protein, lactose, somatic cell count (SCC), thermoduric count
- **Compliance**:
  - Grade-free milk: Farmers penalised for antibiotic residues, high SCC, inhibitory substances
  - DairyNZ Good Farming Practice guidelines
  - Animal welfare: Code of Welfare for Dairy Cattle

## Meat Schedule Prices

- **Major processors**: Silver Fern Farms, Alliance Group, ANZCO Foods, Affco, Progressive Meats, Taylor Preston
- **Schedule components**:
  - Per kg (carcass weight) by grade and weight range
  - Premiums: Organic, grass-fed, specific market programmes
  - Deductions: Cartage, levies (Beef+Lamb NZ)
- **Lamb grades**: Y (young), YX (young crossbred), P (prime), T (hogget)
- **Beef grades**: P (prime steer/heifer), M (manufacturing cow/bull), O (ox)
- **Timing signals**: Schedule movements indicate processor demand; rising schedule = consider timing kill; falling = lock in contracts early

## Weather-to-Operations Mapping

| Forecast | Operations Impact |
|----------|------------------|
| Heavy rain (>25mm) | Bring stock off hills; delay effluent spreading; check flood-prone paddocks; monitor river crossings |
| Frost (-2°C+) | Delay milking start (teat damage); check water troughs; break-feed brassica crops |
| Strong wind (>60km/h) | Secure shelters; delay spraying; check power lines on farm |
| Drought (soil moisture deficit >80mm) | Activate drought management plan; reduce stocking rate; apply for adverse events funding (MPI) |
| Snow | Lamb survival protocols; supplementary feeding; check access roads |
| Flood warning | Move stock to high ground; sandbag effluent ponds; notify regional council if discharge risk |

## Rural Wellbeing

- **Farmstrong**: National rural wellbeing programme
- **High-stress seasons**: Calving (Jul-Sep), lambing (Aug-Oct), mating, drought, flood
- **Key resources**:
  - Farmstrong.co.nz — resilience tools
  - 1737 — NZ Mental Health line
  - Rural Support Trust — regional support
  - Rural Women NZ — community connection
- **Warning signs**: Isolation, sleep disruption, withdrawal from community, substance use increase
- **Check-in approach**: Non-intrusive, normalised, linked to seasonal pressures

## Farm Succession

- **Common structures**: Family trust, limited partnership, company, joint venture
- **Key considerations**:
  - Relationship property implications
  - Bright-line test for farmland (currently 2 years for non-new-build)
  - RLWT (Residential Land Withholding Tax) — generally N/A for working farms
  - GST on farmland sale (if registered)
  - Overseas Investment Act — OIO consent if purchaser is overseas person
  - Māori freehold land — special succession rules under Te Ture Whenua Māori Act 1993
- **Planning timeline**: Typically 5-10 year process
