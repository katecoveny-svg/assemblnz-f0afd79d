## Act overview

**Full title:** Health and Safety at Work Act 2015 (HSWA)
**Commencement:** 4 April 2016
**Administered by:** WorkSafe New Zealand
**Primary kete:** WAIHANGA (construction), all kete (general duties)
**Agent:** ĀRAI (H&S safety)

---

## Key concepts

### PCBU (Person Conducting a Business or Undertaking)
- s17 HSWA — the primary duty holder
- Includes sole traders, companies, partnerships, government departments
- Does NOT include volunteer associations with no employees
- Multiple PCBUs can share duties on the same site (s34 overlapping duties)

### Primary duty of care — s36 HSWA
- Ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, health and safety of workers
- Applies to workers AND others influenced by the work (visitors, public)
- Duty is NOT transferable — cannot be contracted away

### Workers — s19 HSWA
- Broader than "employees" — includes contractors, subcontractors, labour hire, volunteers, trainees
- All workers have duty to take reasonable care (s45)

### Officers — s44 HSWA
- Directors, partners, persons who exercise significant influence over the business
- Must exercise due diligence: understand hazards, ensure resources, verify compliance

## Notifiable events — s24-s26 HSWA

### s25 — What must be notified to WorkSafe
1. **Death** of any person (worker or other)
2. **Notifiable injury or illness** (s23):
   - Amputation
   - Serious head injury
   - Serious eye injury
   - Serious burn
   - Spinal injury
   - Loss of bodily function
   - Serious laceration
   - Medical treatment within 48 hours of exposure to a substance
3. **Notifiable incident** (s24) — dangerous incident, even if no injury:
   - Uncontrolled escape of gas/steam/pressurised substance
   - Uncontrolled fall of plant/object/substance
   - Collapse of structure, excavation, or ground
   - Electric shock
   - Entrapment — person trapped by equipment, cave-in, trench collapse
   - Inrush of water/gas in underground excavation
   - Implosion, explosion, fire
   - Exposure to live electricity within 4m (s22 Electricity Regulations)

### Notification timeline
- Notify WorkSafe **as soon as possible** after becoming aware
- Phone: **0800 030 040**
- Preserve scene until authorised by WorkSafe inspector (s43)
- Written notification within **48 hours** (PCBU duty)

## Hazard management — construction-specific

### Hazard register requirements
- Must be maintained for every workplace (regulation 9, General Risk Regulations 2016)
- Identifies hazards, assesses risks, describes controls
- Must be reviewed when: new work begins, incident occurs, new information available
- **ĀRAI agent auto-logs hazards to action_queue with risk classification**

### Risk hierarchy — regulation 6
1. Eliminate the hazard
2. Minimise risk by substitution, isolation, engineering controls
3. Minimise risk by administrative controls (training, procedures)
4. PPE as last resort

### High-risk construction work — regulation 26
Work requiring SWMS (Safe Work Method Statement):
- Work at height > 5 metres
- Demolition
- Trenching/excavation > 1.5m deep
- Work near live services (electrical, gas, water)
- Confined space entry
- Work involving asbestos
- Use of explosives
- Diving work

## Worker participation — s58-s73 HSWA

### Health and Safety Representative (HSR) — s62-s69
- Elected by workers (not appointed by PCBU)
- Right to inspect workplace, attend interviews, issue provisional improvement notices
- Must receive training within 6 months of election

### Health and Safety Committee — s66-s73
- Required if requested by HSR or 5+ workers
- Meets at least quarterly
- PCBU must support and resource

## Penalties

| Offence | Max fine (organisation) | Max fine (individual) | Max imprisonment |
|---------|------------------------|----------------------|------------------|
| Reckless conduct (s47) | $3,000,000 | $600,000 | 5 years |
| Failure to comply with duty (s48) | $1,500,000 | $300,000 | — |
| Failure to comply with duty exposing to risk (s49) | $500,000 | $100,000 | — |

## Regulations under HSWA relevant to WAIHANGA

### Health and Safety at Work (General Risk and Workplace Management) Regulations 2016
- Regulation 5-12: Risk management duties
- Regulation 9: Hazard register
- Regulation 14-25: Workplace facilities and general duties
- Regulation 26-29: Construction-specific provisions

### Health and Safety at Work (Scaffolding) Regulations 2021
- Scaffolding over 5m requires licensed scaffolder
- Scaffold tags must be current — inspection every 7 days or after adverse weather
- **ĀRAI monitors scaffold_expiry in shared_context**

### Asbestos Regulations 2016
- Asbestos management plan required for PCBUs managing or controlling workplaces
- Asbestos removal: Class A licence for friable, Class B for non-friable > 10m²
