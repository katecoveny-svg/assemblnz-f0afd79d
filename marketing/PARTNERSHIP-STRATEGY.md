# Assembl Partnership & Revenue Acceleration Strategy

**Document Owner:** Kate Hudson, Founder & CEO
**Last Updated:** 25 March 2026
**Status:** Active — Execution Phase
**Confidentiality:** Internal Use Only

---

## Executive Summary

Assembl is Aotearoa New Zealand's first AI operating system — 42 purpose-built AI agents across 16 industries, starting at $89/mo NZD. This document outlines a partnership strategy designed to accelerate revenue through industry bodies, enterprise customers, and technology integrations.

**Target:** $500K–$2M in partnership-driven ARR within 12 months.

**Core thesis:** NZ industry associations collectively represent hundreds of thousands of businesses. A single partnership with one major association can unlock thousands of paying users overnight. We go where the members already are.

## REVENUE MODELS FOR PARTNERSHIPS

### 1. White-Label Licensing

- Partner pays $5,000–$50,000/year for a branded version of a specific agent.
- Agent is reskinned with partner's logo, colours, and domain.
- Partner controls pricing to their end users.
- Assembl provides the platform, updates, and support infrastructure.
- **Best for:** Industry associations, large corporates, banks.

### 2. Revenue Share (Referral)

- Partner refers users via a tracked link or code.
- Partner receives 20–30% of the referred user's monthly subscription for the lifetime of the subscription.
- No upfront cost for the partner.
- **Best for:** Accountants, consultants, web developers, small associations.

### 3. Co-Branded Freemium

- Free access to a specific agent for all association members (basic tier).
- Upsell to paid tiers with advanced features.
- Association gets credit ("Powered by Assembl, exclusive to [Association] members").
- **Best for:** Coastguard NZ, Community Law Centres, DairyNZ.

### 4. Enterprise Site Licence

- Organisation pays $10,000–$100,000/year for unlimited access across their team or client base.
- Custom configuration, onboarding, and dedicated support.
- Annual contract with renewal escalation (3–5% per year).
- **Best for:** Fletcher Building, Fonterra, THL, Ports of Auckland.

### 5. API Integration Fee

- Partners pay for API access to embed Assembl agents in their own platforms.
- Pricing: $500–$5,000/month depending on volume.
- Assembl provides API documentation, sandbox environment, and technical support.
- **Best for:** Xero, MYOB, Trimble, construction management platforms.

### 6. Sponsorship & Co-Marketing

- "APEX powered by Assembl" at industry events and in publications.
- Assembl sponsors industry awards or conferences in exchange for brand exposure and lead generation.
- Pricing: $5,000–$25,000 per event or campaign.
- **Best for:** Master Builders House of the Year, Hospitality NZ Awards, maritime safety campaigns.

|---|---|
| Partner wants exclusivity across too broad a category | Offer exclusivity within their specific sub-sector only (e.g., "exclusive AI partner for Hospitality NZ members" but not "exclusive for all NZ hospitality") |

| Reputational risk if AI gives wrong advice | Maintain clear disclaimers on all agents. Ensure ANCHOR states it is not legal advice. Ensure LEDGER states it is not financial advice. Professional indemnity insurance. |
| Partner relationship goes stale after signing | Quarterly business reviews with every Tier 1 partner. Monthly usage reports. Dedicated partner success manager (hire when revenue justifies it). |
| Competitor launches similar NZ-focused AI | Move fast. First-mover advantage in NZ is real — lock in exclusive partnerships before competitors arrive. |
