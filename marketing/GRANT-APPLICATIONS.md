### Section 3: R&D Project Description

**Project Title:** Development of NZ-Specific AI Business Intelligence Agents with Voice Interface and Regulatory Knowledge Base

**Project Duration:** 24 months (April 2026 -- March 2028)

**Scientific or Technological Uncertainty:**

The core R&D challenge is developing AI agents that can reliably interpret and apply New Zealand-specific legislation and regulatory frameworks across 16 distinct industries, with accuracy sufficient for commercial business guidance. Key uncertainties include:

1. **Regulatory accuracy at scale:** Can large language models be fine-tuned or augmented (via retrieval-augmented generation) to provide consistently accurate guidance across dozens of NZ statutes and regulations, without hallucination or misinterpretation? Each industry has overlapping and sometimes conflicting regulatory requirements.

2. **Voice AI for professional advisory:** Can voice-based AI agents deliver complex regulatory and business guidance conversationally while maintaining accuracy and appropriate disclaimers? The ElevenLabs voice technology must be integrated with real-time knowledge retrieval systems that maintain context across multi-turn conversations.

3. **Cross-industry knowledge architecture:** Can a single platform architecture serve 43+ specialist agents across 16 industries without knowledge contamination (where one agent's training data incorrectly influences another's outputs)? This requires novel approaches to agent isolation and knowledge boundary management.

4. **NZ-specific data pipeline:** Unlike US/UK markets with abundant training data, NZ regulatory content is limited. Can we develop automated pipelines to ingest, structure, and maintain current NZ legislation, case law, and regulatory guidance at sufficient quality for commercial AI advisory use?

**Proposed R&D Activities:**

Phase 1 (Months 1-8): Knowledge Base Development
- Build structured NZ legislative and regulatory knowledge base across all 16 target industries
- Develop automated ingestion pipelines for NZ legislation updates
- Design and test retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) architecture for regulatory accuracy
- Benchmark accuracy against human expert baseline across sample regulatory queries

Phase 2 (Months 6-16): Agent Specialisation and Voice Integration
- Develop fine-tuning approaches for industry-specific agent behaviour
- Integrate ElevenLabs voice AI with real-time knowledge retrieval
- Build and test multi-turn conversational flows for complex advisory scenarios
- Develop agent isolation architecture to prevent cross-industry knowledge contamination

Phase 3 (Months 14-24): Validation, Testing, and Iteration
- Conduct accuracy validation with industry practitioners across all 16 sectors
- Develop automated testing frameworks for regulatory accuracy monitoring
- Build feedback loops for continuous accuracy improvement
- Test and iterate voice interface for professional usability

**Expected Outcomes:**
- A validated AI advisory platform with demonstrated regulatory accuracy across 16 NZ industries
- Novel RAG architecture specifically designed for small-market regulatory knowledge bases
- Published accuracy benchmarks for NZ-specific AI business advisory
- A commercially viable SaaS platform serving NZ SMEs

# APPLICATION 2: RDTI

## Programme Details
- **Administering body:** MBIE and Inland Revenue (jointly)
- **Benefit:** 15% tax credit on eligible R&D expenditure
- **Minimum spend:** $50,000/year on eligible R&D
- **Maximum claim:** $120 million/year (not relevant for Assembl)
- **Cash refund:** Available if in tax loss position (capped at labour-related taxes paid)
- **Website:** https://www.rdti.govt.nz/
- **IRD page:** https://www.ird.govt.nz/research-and-development/tax-incentive

## Key Dates
- **General Approval (GA) due:** Last day of 3rd month after income year ends. For a 31 March year-end, GA is due by 30 June.
- **Supplementary Return due:** 30 days after income tax return due date.

## How to Apply
1. Complete eligibility quizzes at rdti.govt.nz
2. Register for the RDTI
3. Submit General Approval (GA) application (free, provides certainty)
4. At year-end, submit R&D Supplementary Return with IR

Assembl is conducting research and development to create an AI-powered business intelligence platform that applies New Zealand-specific legislative and regulatory knowledge across 16 industries through 43 specialist AI agents.

**Core R&D Activity 1: NZ Regulatory Knowledge Base Architecture**

Objective: Develop a structured knowledge base and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) system that can accurately retrieve and apply NZ-specific legislation across multiple industries.

Systematic approach: Iterative testing of agent isolation methods including separate embedding spaces, prompt-level domain restriction, and dynamic knowledge boundary enforcement. Measured by cross-domain contamination rate in blind testing.

**Core R&D Activity 3: Voice AI Integration for Regulatory Advisory**

Objective: Integrate real-time voice AI (ElevenLabs) with knowledge retrieval systems to deliver conversational regulatory guidance.

Technological uncertainty: It is uncertain whether current voice AI technology can maintain sufficient context across multi-turn conversations about complex regulatory topics while simultaneously performing real-time knowledge retrieval. Latency, accuracy, and conversational coherence must all be maintained simultaneously.

Systematic approach: Controlled testing of different voice-to-text-to-retrieval-to-voice pipelines. Measured by response accuracy, latency, and user comprehension scores.
