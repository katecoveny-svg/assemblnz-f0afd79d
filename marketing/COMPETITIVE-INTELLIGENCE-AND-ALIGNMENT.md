# PART 2: HUMAN-CENTRED AI POSITIONING

## 2.1 What "Human in the Loop" Means in Practice

Human-in-the-loop (HITL) has evolved from basic oversight to strategic partnership. In 2025-2026, HITL means:

- **Decision Validation:** AI generates recommendations; humans make final calls on consequential decisions.
- **Exception Management:** AI handles routine workflows; humans manage edge cases and novel situations.
- **Contextual Judgment:** AI processes data; humans apply cultural, ethical, and relationship context.
- **Continuous Improvement:** Humans provide feedback that refines AI performance over time.
- **Strategic Direction:** AI executes tasks; humans set goals, priorities, and values.

According to Google's framework, HITL operates at three levels: Human-in-the-Loop (human reviews every AI output), Human-on-the-Loop (human monitors but AI acts autonomously within boundaries), and Human-in-Command (human sets strategic parameters and retains override authority).

## 2.2 Companies Successfully Positioning AI as a Team Member

- **Nike:** Built an AI shopping assistant in 21 days with human oversight from day one. Non-technical staff monitor the AI via dashboards. Detailed audit logs and controls ensure accountability.
- **Starbucks:** Human-in-the-loop personalisation grew Rewards membership 13% year-on-year to 34.3 million members. Humans became more strategic, not redundant.

## 2.3 Language and Framing That Reduces AI Anxiety

Research from the World Economic Forum, Fortune, and industry leaders identifies these principles:

**Do say:**
- "AI assistant" or "AI teammate" -- not "AI replacement" or "automation"
- "Augments your expertise" -- not "does it for you"
- "Saves time on admin so you can focus on what matters" -- positions AI as liberating
- "Your knowledge guides the AI" -- reinforces human value
- "AI handles the routine; you handle the relationships" -- clear role separation
- "Built to support your mahi (work)" -- NZ/Maori framing

**Do not say:**
- "Replaces the need for..." -- triggers job loss anxiety
- "Fully autonomous" -- implies humans are unnecessary
- "AI-powered decision making" -- suggests humans are removed from decisions
- "Disruption" or "transformation" without context -- creates fear

**The Five C's Framework (Thoughtworks):**
1. **Competence:** Develop AI literacy so people guide the AI, not blindly follow it.
2. **Collaboration:** AI is a team effort, built from the ground up.
3. **Communication:** Help everyone understand what AI does and does not do.
4. **Creativity:** Free humans for creative and strategic work.
5. **Conscience:** Embed ethics, transparency, and accountability.

## 2.4 NZ-Specific Attitudes to AI in the Workplace

New Zealand presents a unique adoption profile:

- **87% business AI adoption** in 2025 (up from 48% in 2023) -- among the highest globally.
- **84% of NZ knowledge workers** use generative AI -- one of the top countries globally.
- **Only 34% of consumers trust AI** -- a significant trust gap between usage and confidence.
- **Only 24% of NZ workers** have undertaken AI training (vs 39% globally).
- **81% of New Zealanders** believe AI regulation is required.
- **89%** want laws to combat AI-generated misinformation.
- **70% of NZ exports** come from agriculture, horticulture, seafood, and forestry -- sectors requiring physical and relational skills AI cannot replicate.

**Key insight for Assembl:** New Zealanders use AI but do not fully trust it. They want regulation, transparency, and education. Assembl should position itself as the trusted, regulated, NZ-specific AI platform that addresses this trust gap.

The KPMG/University of Melbourne study found NZ has among the lowest AI training levels globally. Assembl can fill this gap by embedding education and guidance into every agent interaction.

## 2.5 Maori Perspectives on AI and Technology

Maori perspectives on AI are nuanced and must be respected:

**Core Principles:**
- **Kaitiakitanga (guardianship):** Who controls Maori data? Maori data sovereignty requires embedding te ao Maori into AI development and supporting Maori-governed research.
- **Mana motuhake (self-determination):** Each of over 100 iwi has its own dialects and knowledge systems. AI cannot flatten this diversity.
- **Wairua and mauri:** Some believe AI lacks the spiritual essence (wairua) and life force (mauri) that give meaning to Maori traditions. Others see potential in language revitalisation.
- **Taonga (treasure):** Matauranga Maori (Maori knowledge) is a taonga. Its digital use must be governed by the people who hold it.

**Key NZ Maori AI Initiatives:**
- **Te Hiku Media:** Maori language AI with 92% accuracy, built exclusively from consented material. Their bespoke "kaitiakitanga licence" prohibits discrimination, surveillance, or tracking.
- **University of Auckland:** $1M AI pronunciation coaching tool for te reo Maori learners.
- **kahu.code:** Bilingual text and voice models supporting indigenous language revitalisation.
- **Sir Hirini Moko Mead's Five Tests:** A Maori framework for assessing contentious issues, applied to data-driven systems.

**Recommendation for Assembl:** Engage with Maori data sovereignty leaders (e.g., Dr Karaitiana Taiuru, Sara Cole Stratton of Maori Lab) before making claims about te reo or tikanga capabilities. Consider a Maori Advisory Board. Use te reo respectfully and in consultation with iwi. Frame AI as a tool that supports Maori aspirations, not one that appropriates Maori knowledge.
