**Heading:** What's Happening?
**Body:** From 1 April 2026, the minimum KiwiSaver contribution rate rises from 3% to 3.5%. This applies to both employee AND employer contributions. It's the first increase since KiwiSaver launched.

### Slide 3
**Heading:** What It Means for Your Pay
**Body:** If you earn $70,000/year, your contributions go from $2,100 to $2,450 per year. That's about $6.70 less per week in take-home pay -- but $350 more going into your retirement fund every year.

### Slide 4
**Heading:** Your Employer Pays More Too
**Body:** Your employer's minimum contribution also goes up to 3.5%. That means an extra $350/year going into YOUR KiwiSaver -- money you never see leave your wallet.

### Slide 5
**Heading:** Should You Contribute More Than the Minimum?
**Body:** The minimum is just the floor, not the ceiling. Contributing 4%, 6%, 8%, or 10% can make a huge difference over time -- especially if you're under 40. Every dollar compounds for decades.

### Slide 6
**Heading:** The Compounding Effect
**Body:** A 30-year-old earning $70K who increases from 3.5% to 6% could have an extra $120,000+ by retirement. That's the power of starting now and staying consistent.

### Slide 7
**Heading:** Check Your Fund Type

### Slide 8
**Heading:** Don't Forget the Government Contribution
**Body:** If you contribute at least $1,042.86/year (about $20/week), the government tops up your KiwiSaver by $521.43. That's free money. Make sure you're getting the full amount.

## CAROUSEL 4: Your Tenancy Rights Have Changed -- Here's What You Need to Know

### Slide 1 (Cover)
**Heading:** Your Tenancy Rights Have Changed
**Subheading:** What every NZ renter needs to know right now
**Visual:** Neon emerald house/key icon on dark background. HELM logo bottom-right.

### Slide 2
**Heading:** 90-Day No-Cause Terminations Are Back
**Body:** Landlords can once again end a periodic tenancy with 90 days' notice without giving a reason. This reversed the previous ban on no-cause terminations. Know your rights -- you still have protections during a fixed-term tenancy.

### Slide 3
**Heading:** Fixed-Term vs Periodic -- Know the Difference
**Body:** Fixed-term: set end date, neither party can end early without cause. Periodic: rolls month to month, either party can give notice. If your fixed term ends and you keep paying rent, it automatically becomes periodic.

### Slide 4
**Heading:** Pets in Rentals
**Body:** Landlords can still say no to pets, but if your tenancy agreement doesn't mention pets, you can ask. Some landlords now include pet clauses with conditions (bond top-up, professional cleaning). Always get pet approval in writing.

### Slide 5
**Heading:** Healthy Homes Standards
**Body:** All rental properties must meet Healthy Homes standards for heating, insulation, ventilation, moisture and drainage, and draught stopping. If your home doesn't comply, your landlord must fix it. You can report non-compliance to Tenancy Services.

### Slide 6
**Heading:** Your Bond Rights
**Body:** Maximum bond is 4 weeks' rent. It must be lodged with Tenancy Services within 23 working days. At the end of your tenancy, your landlord has to apply to Tenancy Services for any deductions -- they can't just keep it. You can dispute any claim.

### Slide 7
**Heading:** Rent Increases
**Body:** Rent can only be increased once every 12 months, with at least 60 days' written notice. The increase must be to market rent -- not an arbitrary amount. You can challenge an excessive increase through the Tenancy Tribunal.

### Slide 8
**Heading:** What to Do If Things Go Wrong
**Body:** Step 1: Put your complaint in writing to your landlord. Step 2: Contact Tenancy Services (0800 836 262) for free mediation. Step 3: Apply to the Tenancy Tribunal if mediation fails. Keep records of everything.

**Body:** HELM explains your rights in plain language, helps you draft letters to your landlord, checks whether your home meets Healthy Homes standards, and guides you through the complaints process step by step.

## CAROUSEL 5: School Zoning, NCEA, and Education Choices -- A Parent's Guide

### Slide 1 (Cover)
**Heading:** School Zoning, NCEA & Education Choices
**Subheading:** A parent's guide to navigating NZ schools in 2026
**Visual:** Neon emerald graduation cap icon on dark background. HELM logo bottom-right.

### Slide 2
**Heading:** School Zones Explained
**Body:** Most state schools have an enrolment zone. If you live in-zone, your child has a guaranteed place. Out-of-zone students enter a ballot -- there's no guarantee. Check your school's zone on the Ministry of Education website or ask HELM.

### Slide 3
**Heading:** New Attendance Rules from January 2026
**Body:** Schools now have stricter attendance management plans. If your child's attendance drops below 70%, the school must intervene with a formal plan. Regular attendance (90%+) is linked to significantly better outcomes at every level.

### Slide 4
**Heading:** What the Attendance Thresholds Mean
**Body:** 90%+ = Regular attender. 80-90% = Irregular -- the school will contact you. 70-80% = Moderate absence -- a formal meeting and plan. Below 70% = Chronic absence -- intensive intervention and potentially involvement from attendance services.

### Slide 5
**Heading:** NCEA Changes You Need to Know
**Body:** The NCEA Change Programme is rolling out progressively. New achievement standards are being introduced with a focus on fewer, larger assessments. Literacy and numeracy co-requisites are now required to achieve NCEA Level 1.

### Slide 6
**Heading:** Understanding NCEA Credits
**Body:** Level 1: 60 credits needed + literacy and numeracy co-requisite. Level 2: 60 credits at Level 2 or above. Level 3: 60 credits at Level 3 or above. University Entrance: NCEA Level 3 + 14 credits in 3 approved subjects + literacy and numeracy.

### Slide 7
**Heading:** Beyond NCEA -- Other Pathways
**Body:** NCEA isn't the only option. Trades academies, Gateway programmes, and vocational pathways offer hands-on learning from Year 11. International Baccalaureate (IB) and Cambridge are available at some schools. Every pathway can lead to a good career.

### Slide 8
**Heading:** Choosing the Right School
**Body:** Consider: zone availability, ERO reports (school reviews), NCEA results, extracurricular offerings, special character (religious, language immersion), and what matters most to your child. Visit schools during open days and talk to other parents.

**Heading:** What HELM Can Help With
**Body:** HELM can check school zones for your address, explain NCEA credit requirements, break down attendance rules, compare schools using ERO data, and help you understand the full range of education pathways available.

## CAROUSEL 6: NZ Tax Credits You're Probably Missing

### Slide 1 (Cover)
**Heading:** NZ Tax Credits You're Probably Missing
**Subheading:** Money back in your pocket -- if you know where to look
**Visual:** Neon emerald receipt/tax icon on dark background. HELM logo bottom-right.

### Slide 2
**Heading:** 1. Independent Earner Tax Credit (IETC)
**Body:** Earn between $24,000 and $48,000? You could get up to $520/year back. It's automatic through your employer if you're on a tax code ME -- but many people are on the wrong code and miss out entirely.

### Slide 3
**Heading:** How to Check Your IETC
**Body:** Log into myIR and check your tax code. If you earn $24,000-$48,000, aren't receiving Working for Families or a benefit, and your tax code isn't ME, you might be missing out. You can claim it back through a Personal Tax Summary.

### Slide 4
**Heading:** 2. Working for Families Tax Credits
**Body:** Four types: Family Tax Credit (main one), In-Work Tax Credit (extra $50/week from April 2026), Minimum Family Tax Credit, and Parental Tax Credit. Combined, these can be worth over $10,000/year for a family with two kids.

### Slide 5
**Heading:** 3. Donation Tax Credits
**Body:** Donated to a registered charity, school, church, or community organisation? You can claim back 33.33% of your donations up to the amount of your taxable income. Gave $300 to your kid's school? That's $100 back.

### Slide 6
**Heading:** How to Claim Donation Credits
**Body:** Keep your donation receipts. Log into myIR and submit a Donation Tax Credit claim (IR526). You have up to 4 years to claim, so check if you missed previous years. School donations, church tithes, and charity koha all count.

### Slide 7
**Heading:** 4. Residential Solar Power Exemption
**Body:** If you generate solar power at home and sell the excess back to the grid, that income is tax-free up to $10,000/year. This applies from 1 April 2024 onwards. No need to declare it -- it's an automatic exemption for residential properties.

### Slide 8
**Heading:** 5. PIE Tax Rate Advantages
**Body:** If your KiwiSaver or managed fund uses a PIE structure, your maximum tax rate is 28% -- even if your personal rate is 33% or 39%. Check your PIE rate is set correctly. Many Kiwis are overtaxed on their investments because of an incorrect rate.
