## 1. AURA - Hospitality

**Covers:** Hotels, restaurants, cafes, bars, food trucks, catering, tourism operators

### A) Top 5 NZ Laws/Regulations

1. **Food Act 2014 & Food Regulations 2015** - Risk-based food safety regime administered by MPI. Businesses with higher food safety risk operate under more stringent checks. Food safety plans (template or custom) are mandatory depending on risk level.
2. **Sale and Supply of Alcohol Act 2012 (as amended 2026)** - Governs all alcohol licensing. The Sale and Supply of Alcohol (Improving Alcohol Regulation) Amendment Act 2026 introduces streamlined licensing, allows zero-alcohol products to meet licensing requirements, and reforms objection procedures. On/off/club/special licences and manager certificates apply.
3. **Health and Safety at Work Act 2015** - PCBUs (Persons Conducting a Business or Undertaking) must manage workplace risks. Hospitality-specific risks include burns, slips, knife injuries, fatigue from long hours. The 2026 Amendment Bill simplifies obligations for small low-risk businesses.
4. **Residential Tenancies (Healthy Homes Standards) Regulations 2019** - For accommodation providers operating residential-style accommodation, heating, insulation, ventilation, moisture/drainage, and draught standards apply. Full compliance deadline was 1 July 2025.
5. **Building Act 2004** - Fire safety, accessibility, and building compliance for hospitality premises. The Building and Construction (Small Stand-alone Dwellings) Amendment Act 2025 enables granny flats up to 70m2 without consent (relevant for Airbnb/holiday lets).

### B) Top 3 Industry Pain Points

1. **Licensing & Compliance Overload** - Navigating food safety plans, alcohol licensing renewals, health & safety requirements, and building compliance simultaneously. The Ministry for Regulation has launched a dedicated hospitality regulatory review to address red tape.
2. **Workforce Shortage** - 63% drop in tourism-related tertiary students over 10 years, 73% decrease in hospitality course completions. Staff scheduling, visa compliance for migrant workers, and training are constant challenges.
3. **Multi-Platform Revenue Management** - Juggling bookings across Booking.com, Airbnb, direct channels, POS systems, and accounting, with GST compliance at 15% and payday filing obligations.

## 2. MARINER - Maritime

**Covers:** Commercial shipping, fishing vessels, port operations, coast guard, recreational boating

### A) Top 5 NZ Laws/Regulations

1. **Maritime Transport Act 1994** - Primary statute governing maritime safety, marine environment protection, ship registration, and the role of Maritime New Zealand. Provides for maritime rules and marine protection rules as secondary legislation.
2. **Fisheries Act 1996 & Fisheries (Commercial Fishing) Regulations 2001 (amended 2025)** - Quota Management System (QMS), catch reporting, deemed values, and commercial fishing vessel requirements. The 2025 amendment updated commercial fishing regulations.
3. **Health and Safety at Work (Adventure Activities) Regulations 2016** - Applies to commercial maritime adventure activities (jet boating, kayaking, sailing charters). Full review scheduled for 2026.
4. **Maritime Rules 40-Series (2026 reform)** - Rules governing domestic commercial vessel design, construction, and equipment. Parts 40A, 40C (non-SOLAS non-passenger ships), 40D (fishing ships), and 40E (sailing ships) updated January 2026. STCW-F Convention changes entered force 1 January 2026.
5. **Resource Management Act 1991 (being replaced 2026)** - Coastal permits, marine discharge consents, and environmental compliance. Being replaced by Planning Bill and Natural Environment Bill, expected to pass in 2026.

### B) Top 3 Industry Pain Points

1. **Crew Certification & Compliance Tracking** - STCW-F Convention changes (1 January 2026) introduced new harmonised qualification standards. Tracking crew certifications, medical fitness, sea time, and ongoing training requirements across multiple crew members is complex.
2. **Vessel Safety Compliance** - The 40-series reform and RAP 3 (upcoming consultation) create constantly evolving safety requirements for vessel design, equipment, and operations. Port and Harbour Marine Safety Code 2026 adds port-specific requirements.
3. **Catch Reporting & Quota Management** - Real-time catch reporting, deemed value calculations, and ACE (Annual Catch Entitlement) management under the QMS is data-intensive. Penalties for misreporting are severe.

## 3. TERRA - Agriculture

**Covers:** Dairy, sheep & beef, horticulture, viticulture, cropping, forestry

### A) Top 5 NZ Laws/Regulations

1. **Biosecurity Act 1993** - World-first biosecurity legislation administered by MPI's Biosecurity New Zealand. Governs border controls, pest management, and unwanted organism responses. Recent events include HPAI H7N6 eradication (2024-25) and yellow-legged hornet incursion in Auckland.
2. **Climate Change Response Act 2002 (as amended 2024)** - Agricultural activities removed from the NZ ETS by the 2024 Amendment Act. Farmers no longer report on emissions from 1 January 2026 and will not pay for emissions from 1 January 2027. Government intends farm-level pricing by 2030.
3. **Resource Management Act 1991 (being replaced)** - Freshwater regulations, National Environmental Standards for Freshwater, and discharge consents. Being replaced by Planning Bill and Natural Environment Bill in 2026, with the new system operational from 2029.
4. **Animal Welfare Act 1999** - Minimum standards for care of animals, codes of welfare for different species (dairy cattle, layer hens, pigs, etc.). Administered by MPI.
5. **Dairy Industry Restructuring Act 2001 (amended 2025)** - Governs Fonterra's structure, raw milk supply obligations, and export licence allocation. The 2025 amendment updated export licence allocation for designated markets.

### B) Top 3 Industry Pain Points

1. **Freshwater Compliance & Environmental Regulations** - Nitrogen and phosphorus limits, winter grazing rules, stock exclusion requirements, and nutrient management plans. The RMA replacement will change the framework but uncertainty exists during the transition period.
2. **Emissions Reporting Uncertainty** - While the ETS obligations are removed, the government plans farm-level pricing by 2030. Farmers need to prepare for on-farm emissions measurement capabilities and future pricing mechanisms.
3. **Biosecurity Threats & Market Access** - Maintaining pest-free status for export markets (fruit fly-free, FMD-free, BSE-free). HPAI incursion in 2024 showed vulnerability. Export certificates and phytosanitary requirements are complex.

## 4. HAVEN - Property/Real Estate

**Covers:** Real estate agents, property investors, property managers, developers

### A) Top 5 NZ Laws/Regulations

1. **Real Estate Agents Act 2008** - Governs licensing of real estate agents, administered by the Real Estate Authority (REA). Amended November 2025 and January 2026 to align with vocational training changes. CPD requirements mandatory. Regulatory Systems (Occupational Regulation) Amendment Bill addresses penalty alignment.
2. **Residential Tenancies Act 1986 (amended 2024/2025)** - Major 2025 changes: 90-day no-cause termination restored, 21-day tenant notice period, pet provisions (pet bond up to 2 weeks rent), Healthy Homes Standards mandatory from 1 July 2025 (penalties up to $7,200). Tribunal can decide proceedings on papers from March 2025. Electronic service of notices now permitted.
3. **Overseas Investment Act 2005 (amended December 2025)** - Foreign buyer ban partially lifted for Active Investor Plus visa holders buying property over NZ$5 million. Applications processed within 5 working days, fees $2,040 (existing) / $3,500 (new build). Broad foreign buyer restriction remains for all others.
4. **Income Tax Act 2007 - Bright-Line Test** - Reduced to 2 years from 1 July 2024 (previously 10 years). Full interest deductibility restored from 1 April 2025. Main home exemption applies if certain use criteria met. Business premises and farmland excluded.
5. **Building Act 2004 & Building and Construction (Small Stand-alone Dwellings) Amendment Act 2025** - Granny flats up to 70m2 without building consent from 15 January 2026. Proportionate liability replacing joint and several for construction defects. Overseas building products acceptance improved by 2025 Amendment Act.

### B) Top 3 Industry Pain Points

1. **Tenancy Law Complexity** - Navigating the restored no-cause terminations, new pet provisions, Healthy Homes compliance, bond management, and the upcoming Employment Leave Act (replacing Holidays Act) for staff. Different rules for different tenancy types.
2. **Foreign Investment & Anti-Money Laundering** - AML/CFT obligations for real estate agents are significant. The 2025 Statutes Amendment Act eased address verification for standard CDD but enhanced CDD still requires it. Agents must maintain compliance programmes and report suspicious activities.
3. **Market Valuation & DTI Impact** - Reserve Bank DTI rules (from July 2024) limit borrowing capacity. LVR restrictions, interest rate changes, and bright-line rule changes create complex investment scenarios requiring constant market intelligence.

## 5. LEDGER - Finance/Accounting

**Covers:** Bookkeepers, accountants, tax agents, financial advisors

### A) Top 5 NZ Laws/Regulations

1. **Income Tax Act 2007 (as amended)** - Governs all income tax in NZ. Five tax brackets (10.5%, 17.5%, 30%, 33%, 39%). Taxation (Budget Measures) Act 2025 introduced Investment Boost (20% accelerated depreciation). Full interest deductibility for residential property from 1 April 2025. Bright-line at 2 years.
2. **Goods and Services Tax Act 1985** - 15% GST on most goods and services. Registration mandatory if turnover exceeds $60,000. Returns due 28th of month following taxable period. 2025 changes extend GST refund timeframe for returned imports from 2 to 12 months.
3. **Tax Administration Act 1994** - Governs IRD powers, tax agent obligations, penalties, use-of-money interest (9.89% underpayment / 3.27% overpayment from May 2025), and compliance requirements.
4. **Anti-Money Laundering and Countering Financing of Terrorism Act 2009** - Accountants are reporting entities. 2025 amendments: standard CDD no longer requires address verification, suspicious activity report window increased to 5 working days, prescribed transaction reporting window doubled to 20 working days. DIA becomes single supervisor from July 2026.
5. **KiwiSaver Act 2006** - Minimum contribution rates increasing: 3.5% from 1 April 2026, 4% from 1 April 2028. Employer subsidy excluded for income over $180,000 from 1 July 2025.

### B) Top 3 Industry Pain Points

1. **Tax Complexity & Constant Change** - Tracking annual rate changes, Investment Boost depreciation, non-resident visitor rules (275-day/18-month from April 2026), GloBE/Pillar Two rules, FBT rate changes, and specified contractor tax alignment. The Taxation (Annual Rates 2025-26) Bill adds further amendments.
2. **AML/CFT Compliance Burden** - Customer due diligence, ongoing monitoring, suspicious activity reporting, and annual compliance reporting. Looming omnibus amendment bill (mid-2026) and FATF evaluation preparation require constant attention.
3. **Payday Filing & Payroll Compliance** - Two working day deadline for employment information submission, KiwiSaver contribution changes, ACC levy calculations (1.67% for 2025-26), wage theft criminalisation, and specified contractor classifications.
